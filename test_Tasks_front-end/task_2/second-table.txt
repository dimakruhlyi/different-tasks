                <p><strong>Sacramento</strong>&nbsp;(<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a title="Help:IPA/English" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English">/<span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/&aelig;/: 'a' in 'bad'">&aelig;</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span>/</a></span></span>&nbsp;<a title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key"><em title="English pronunciation respelling">SAK-rə-MEN-toh</em></a>;&nbsp;<small>Spanish:&nbsp;</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a title="Help:IPA/Spanish" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish">[sakɾaˈmento]</a></span>;&nbsp;<a title="Spanish language" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language">Spanish</a>&nbsp;for "sacrament") is the&nbsp;<a title="Capital city" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_city">capital city</a>&nbsp;of the U.S. state of&nbsp;<a title="California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California">California</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="County seat" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_seat">seat</a>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<a title="Sacramento County, California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_County,_California">Sacramento County</a>. Located at the confluence of the&nbsp;<a title="Sacramento River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_River">Sacramento River</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="American River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_River">American River</a>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<a title="Northern California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_California">Northern California</a>'s&nbsp;<a title="Sacramento Valley" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_Valley">Sacramento Valley</a>, Sacramento's estimated 2018 population of 501,334 makes it the&nbsp;<a title="List of largest California cities by population" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_California_cities_by_population">sixth-largest city in California</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="List of United States cities by population" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_United_States_cities_by_population">ninth largest capital in the United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>&nbsp;Sacramento is the seat of the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="California Legislature" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislature">California Legislature</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="Governor of California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governor_of_California">Governor of California</a>, making it the state's political center and a hub for&nbsp;<a title="Lobbying" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lobbying">lobbying</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Think tanks" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Think_tanks">think tanks</a>. Sacramento is also the cultural and economic core of the&nbsp;<a title="Sacramento metropolitan area" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_metropolitan_area">Sacramento metropolitan area</a>, which had a 2010 population of 2,414,783,<sup id="cite_ref-csapop_10-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-csapop-10">[10]</a></sup>&nbsp;making it the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Table of United States Metropolitan Statistical Areas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Table_of_United_States_Metropolitan_Statistical_Areas">fifth largest in California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before the&nbsp;<a title="Spanish colonization of the Americas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas">arrival of the Spanish</a>, the area was inhabited by the&nbsp;<a title="Nisenan" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nisenan">Nisenan</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Indigenous peoples of California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_California">indigenous peoples of California</a>. Spanish cavalryman&nbsp;<a title="Gabriel Moraga" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabriel_Moraga">Gabriel Moraga</a>&nbsp;surveyed and named the&nbsp;<em>Rio del Sant&iacute;simo Sacramento</em>&nbsp;(<a title="Sacramento River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_River">Sacramento River</a>) in 1808, after the&nbsp;<a title="Blessed Sacrament" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blessed_Sacrament">Blessed Sacrament</a>, referring to the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Eucharist (Catholic Church)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eucharist_(Catholic_Church)">Eucharist in the Catholic Church</a>. In 1839,&nbsp;<a title="Juan Bautista Alvarado" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Bautista_Alvarado">Juan Bautista Alvarado</a>,&nbsp;<a title="List of Governors of California before 1850" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_California_before_1850">Mexican governor</a>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<a title="Alta California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alta_California">Alta California</a>&nbsp;granted the responsibility of colonizing the&nbsp;<a title="Sacramento Valley" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento_Valley">Sacramento Valley</a>&nbsp;to&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss">Swiss</a>-born, Mexican citizen&nbsp;<a title="John Sutter" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Sutter">John Augustus Sutter</a>, who subsequently established&nbsp;<a title="Sutter's Fort" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sutter%27s_Fort">Sutter's Fort</a>&nbsp;and the settlement at the&nbsp;<em>Rancho&nbsp;<a title="New Helvetia" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Helvetia">Nueva Helvetia</a>.</em>&nbsp;Following the American&nbsp;<a title="Conquest of California" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conquest_of_California">Conquest of California</a>&nbsp;and the 1848&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Guadalupe-Hidalgo" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Guadalupe-Hidalgo">Treaty of Guadalupe-Hidalgo</a>, the waterfront developed by Sutter began to be developed and incorporated in 1850 as the City of Sacramento. As a result of the&nbsp;<a title="California Gold Rush" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Gold_Rush">California Gold Rush</a>, Sacramento became a major commercial center and distribution point for Northern California, serving as the terminus for the&nbsp;<a title="Pony Express" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pony_Express">Pony Express</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="First Transcontinental Railroad" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Transcontinental_Railroad">First Transcontinental Railroad</a>.</p>
<p>Sacramento is the fastest-growing major city in California,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>&nbsp;owing to its status as a notable&nbsp;<a title="Finance" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finance">financial</a>&nbsp;center on the&nbsp;<a title="West Coast of the United States" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States">West Coast</a>&nbsp;and as a major educational hub, home of&nbsp;<a title="California State University, Sacramento" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_State_University,_Sacramento">Sacramento State University</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="University of California, Davis" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Davis">University of California, Davis</a>. Similarly, Sacramento is a major center for the California&nbsp;<a title="Healthcare industry" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Healthcare_industry">healthcare industry</a>, as the seat of&nbsp;<a title="Sutter Health" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sutter_Health">Sutter Health</a>, the world-renowned&nbsp;<a title="UC Davis Medical Center" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UC_Davis_Medical_Center">UC Davis Medical Center</a>, and the&nbsp;<a title="UC Davis School of Medicine" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UC_Davis_School_of_Medicine">UC Davis School of Medicine</a>, and notable&nbsp;<a title="Tourism" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism">tourist</a>&nbsp;destination in California, as the site of&nbsp;<a title="The California Museum" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_California_Museum">The California Museum</a>, the&nbsp;<a title="Crocker Art Museum" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crocker_Art_Museum">Crocker Art Museum</a>,&nbsp;<a title="California Hall of Fame" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Hall_of_Fame">California Hall of Fame</a>, the&nbsp;<a title="California State Capitol Museum" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_State_Capitol_Museum">California State Capitol Museum</a>, and the&nbsp;<a title="Old Sacramento State Historic Park" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Sacramento_State_Historic_Park">Old Sacramento State Historic Park</a>. Sacramento is known for its evolving contemporary culture, dubbed the most "<a title="Hipster (contemporary subculture)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hipster_(contemporary_subculture)">hipster</a>&nbsp;city" in California.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>&nbsp;In 2002, the&nbsp;<a title="Harvard University" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University">Harvard University</a>&nbsp;Civil Rights Project conducted for&nbsp;<em><a title="Time (magazine)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_(magazine)">Time</a></em>&nbsp;magazine named Sacramento "America's Most Diverse City".<sup id="cite_ref-MostDiverseCity-TIME_17-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacramento,_California#cite_note-MostDiverseCity-TIME-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
